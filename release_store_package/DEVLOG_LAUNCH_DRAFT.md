## FULL DEVLOG BODY

---

PHENIX: NULL EDEN is out now on itch.io.

I built this because I wanted to play it — a cyber-survival roguelite that felt like something. Seven characters. Seven bosses. Three modes. One Chaos Mode that changes everything when you hit 21 minutes Endless.

It's a complete, playable release. Act 1. Endless. Chaos Mode with Survival Rank badges. Relics. Permanent Grid Credit progression. Full controller support. Full touch/mobile support. Original music that shifts when Chaos activates.

---

**What's in the launch version:**

- Act 1 — 21 minute timed survival run
- Endless Mode — no ceiling, build however long you can hold
- Chaos Mode — unlocked at 21:00 Endless. Chaos Laws. Rank badges: BRONZE · SILVER · GOLD · PLATINUM
- 7 playable characters, each with a unique ultimate Protocol
- 7 bosses and mini-bosses
- Relics, level-up upgrades, and Grid Credit meta-progression
- Walker — a companion NPC who appears at key moments across your run
- Full keyboard + mouse, gamepad (PS5/Xbox/PC), and mobile touch support

---

**What's coming after launch (no dates promised):**

- Additional Chaos Laws — Serpent Law, Dragon Law, Broken Signal are in development
- Japan Phasewalker — full character implementation, not a preview slot
- Endgame resource mechanics for players who've cleared the current progression loop
- Balance and polish updates based on real feedback

I'm not going to promise dates. When it's ready, it ships.

---

**On the launch price:**

PHENIX: NULL EDEN is 5.99€ at launch — the supporter rate. The base value is 9.99€. If you buy now, you get the lower price forever and you support continued development. If you wait, it goes back up. That's the deal, stated plainly.

---

**Why itch.io first:**

itch.io is where indie games live. It's the right place to launch first — no storefront noise, direct to players who actually want this kind of game. Steam is in planning. More on that when it's ready.

---

**One honest thing:**

This took longer than I thought. Everything does. But the game is real — it boots, it runs, it has a beginning and an end and a Chaos Mode that earns its name. I'm proud of what it became.

If you play it, let me know how far you got in Chaos Mode.
