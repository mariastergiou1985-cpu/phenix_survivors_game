# PHENIX: NULL EDEN — Cover Candidate 03
## Version: SKELETON WARRIOR BALANCED HERO-VS-BOSS
## Strategy: Variation A composition with alternate hero — darker, more unique identity
## Commercial strength: 7.5/10 — strongest unique identity, best differentiation from genre peers

---

## WHY THIS CANDIDATE

Most cyberpunk roguelite covers use an armored human or generic cyber-warrior. A skeletal cyber-warrior is instantly distinctive — the bone structure creates a silhouette that reads clearly at thumbnail size and sticks in memory. This candidate explores a different mood: darker, more corrupted, "the dead that will not stay down." The NULL EDEN grid is a place of digital corruption — a cyber-necromancer fits that identity more uniquely than a demon warrior.

This is the "unexpected" option. If it lands, it will be more memorable than V1 or V2. If it feels too niche, use V1 for itch and reserve the Skeleton Warrior for secondary assets.

## MAIN PROMPT

```
Premium dark cyberpunk indie game cover key art. Game: "PHENIX: NULL EDEN", a cyber-survival roguelite.

HERO — SKELETON WARRIOR (lower center, 35% of frame height): A skeletal cyberpunk warrior in dark bone-plate armor. The character's core structure is skeletal — ribcage partially visible through gaps in the chest armor, skull partially visible beneath a cracked visor or helmet. But this is not a horror zombie — this is a CYBER-SKELETAL WARRIOR: the bones are reinforced with dark metal plating and glowing neon cyan energy channels running through the joints, spine, and eye sockets. The eye sockets glow cyan — intense, precise, dangerous. The armor is dark matte metal with neon cyan energy veins — where the Oni is demonic, the Skeleton is precise and corrupted. Mid-attack: Bone Guard Blast erupts from both hands — a burst of cyan and gold bone-shard energy, fragmenting outward in a cone toward the upper frame (toward the boss). Full body clearly visible. Stance: grounded, forward-leaning, mid-attack. The character should read as powerful AND eerie — a dead thing that refuses to stop fighting. Strong rim lighting from below: cyan from the ground-level energy, gold from the blast effect. The bone surfaces are lit — not dull white, but lit cyberpunk bone with metallic sheen and neon channels.

BOSS — MATRIX ANNIHILATOR (upper center, 45% of frame height): Same presence as Candidate 01 — looming, massive, angular black geometric machine, crimson eyes blazing, null-red arcs sweeping outward. In this version, the boss's color palette and the hero's color palette create a specific opposition: the boss is RED and BLACK (null, death, machine). The hero is CYAN and BONE (corrupted, undead, digital). The contrast emphasizes that this warrior was already dead before the null entity arrived — and it will outlast it.

COMPOSITION: Same balanced A layout as Candidate 01. Hero lower center. Boss upper center. Open negative space between them as the contested zone. Enemy silhouettes at lower corners, in shadow. The Skeleton Warrior's bone white and cyan glowing joints should create a visually distinct focal point that doesn't require recognition of the character's lore — the silhouette alone communicates "this is the player" at thumbnail size.

BACKGROUND ATMOSPHERE: Same deep void (#04060C). Faint cyan grid lines, atmospheric only. Upper zone: null-red boss aura. Lower zone: cyan-gold hero energy on dark ground. NEW addition for this candidate: occasional floating bone-fragment particles in the air around the hero — very subtle, white-cyan, as if the hero is shedding corrupted data-bone. This is subtle — 5–10 small fragments, not a blizzard.

PARTICLES: Cyan bone-shard fragments from the Bone Guard Blast eruption (pointed fragments, not round orbs). Gold energy trails from the blast cone. Red null-static from the boss descending. Cyan data-shard particles bridge the space between hero and boss. Bone fragments at the hero's feet (residual from the blast, landing on the ground).

COLOR NOTES: The Skeleton Warrior introduces near-white bone surfaces into the palette. These must be LIT: the bone is not flat white — it is illuminated from the cyan energy within and the gold explosion without. At full darkness (unlit surfaces), bone surfaces should grade toward #2a2a2a or similar — dark enough to not break the dark mood. The glowing joint channels are #00f5ff (cyan) — these should be the brightest feature on the hero, not the bone surface itself.

TITLE TYPOGRAPHY — EMBEDDED IN IMAGE: Same placement as Candidate 01. "PHENIX: NULL EDEN" in the top third, metallic gold with cyan glow. "A CYBER-SURVIVAL ROGUELITE" in dim cyan small-caps below. Must be legible at 300px width. The near-white bone elements of the hero should NOT visually compete with the gold title — the title is in the upper frame, the hero is in the lower frame; they should not be confused.

MOOD: The null grid cannot kill what was already dead. The last warrior standing is the one who had nothing left to lose. Dark, eerie, defiant.

STYLE: Same premium dark cyberpunk digital illustration. NOT anime, NOT pixel, NOT mobile cartoon. NOT halloween horror or rotting zombie aesthetic — think "corrupted digital knight" not "undead monster." The Skeleton Warrior is a player character people want to choose, not a monster they fear.

DO NOT INCLUDE: Rotting flesh, decay aesthetic, blood, gore. Generic zombie look. Horror-movie tone. Phasewalker (white/silver female ninja). Any HUD or game UI. Award badges. Crowded frame. Pastel colors. Magenta/pink dominant palette. Bright backgrounds. Anime faces. Any text other than the embedded title. Mobile controls.
```

## COMMERCIAL STRENGTH: 7.5/10

**Strengths:** Highly distinctive. Skeleton warrior silhouette reads at tiny sizes with no detail loss (bone structure = natural high-contrast). The cyber-necromancer aesthetic is underrepresented in roguelite store art — this would stand out. Strong for social sharing (unusual, memorable). Best candidate for the Steam library capsule (600×900 portrait).

**Risks:**
- AI generation quality risk: tools default to zombie horror for skeleton characters. Prompt must strongly resist this. Multiple iterations may be needed.
- Niche appeal: the skeleton warrior is a less immediately "heroic" image than Oni — may feel dark to some audiences who want more uplifting hero energy
- Bone surface + near-white tone risks breaking the dark palette — must be LIT, not flat
- If the cyberpunk armor doesn't read clearly, the character looks like a Halloween decoration

**Best used as:** itch.io cover (if uniqueness is the goal), Steam library capsule (600×900), social preview, alternate campaign art

**If this candidate underperforms:** Use V1 (Oni Balanced) as primary, then add the Skeleton Warrior on a secondary asset (loading screen, trailer segment, character spotlight post).
